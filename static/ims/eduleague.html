EDULEAGUE × EDEN

Pilot Learning Protocol Proposal

Architect: Divine Favour Yusuf Location: Pankshin Node Date: May 2026

01 — The Vision

EduLeague is a structured, short-cycle academic challenge system designed to transform students from "Storage Units" of memorized facts into "Operators" of systemic knowledge. It is a return to foundation—an integrated model where competitive learning, collaborative study, and real-world application merge into a single educational engine.

Phase I: The Challenge Cycle (3–7 Days)

Students form Tutorial Groups (5–10 members) to master a specific topic. The cycle culminates in a Live Defense, where teams must present their findings and withstand structured questioning from opposing peers, audience members, and judges. This process builds: Clarity of Thought, Confidence in Expression, and Collaborative Intelligence.

02 — Subject Application

The protocol applies across all core academic pillars—Mathematics, English, Sciences, and Social Studies. However, the objective shifts from rote memorization to Systemic Defense. Students are graded not just on "Correctness," but on their ability to apply knowledge to real-life examples and demonstrate practical understanding.

The Eden Layer (Real-World Extension)

The Eden Farm Project serves as the physical laboratory for EduLeague. Abstract curriculum is grounded in the red soil of Pankshin through experiential integration:

  • ◈ Biology: Soil ecosystems and plant growth cycles.
  • ◈ Physics: Water flow dynamics and energy resource allocation.
  • ◈ Mathematics: Yield calculations and spatial measurement.
  • ◈ Economics: Farm production value systems and pricing.

03 — Strategic Implementation

The pilot begins as an internal school initiative at Solid Foundation Academy, focusing on selected JSS/SSS classes. As the structure stabilizes, it expands into an Inter-School Season model, eventually revolving around Thematic Knowledge Seasons that ask foundational questions: "What is Value?" "How does a society grow?" "What is Energy?"
